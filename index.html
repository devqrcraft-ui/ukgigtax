Self Assessment

Three Things HMRC Requires

1
Register for Self Assessment
If your gig earnings exceed £1,000 in a tax year, register with HMRC as self-employed. Deadline: 5 October 2026 for the 2025/26 year. Late registration results in penalties that increase over time.
2
Track income and allowable expenses
Keep records of all platform payments and business costs throughout the year. A mileage log and organised receipts will significantly reduce your taxable profit and your tax bill.
3
File and pay by 31 January 2027
Submit your Self Assessment online and pay any tax owed. Set aside the monthly amount shown in the calculator above — transfer it to a separate account every time you are paid to avoid a cash-flow problem at deadline.

Online filing and payment deadline is 31 January each year. For 2025/26 that means 31 January 2027. An automatic £100 penalty applies if you miss the deadline, rising significantly after 3 months.
Class 4 NI at 6% on profits between £12,570 and £50,270, and 2% above. Class 2 NI was abolished from 6 April 2024 and is now voluntary only for those with profits below £7,105. Many online calculators still show the old 9% Class 4 rate, overstating your bill.
Yes. HMRC allows 45p per mile by car or van for the first 10,000 business miles, 24p per mile by motorbike, and 20p per mile by bicycle. Keep a log of date, destination, purpose, and distance for every trip. You cannot claim mileage and also separately claim fuel and servicing.
Your employer taxes your salary through PAYE using your personal allowance. Your gig profit is stacked on top, taxed at whatever band it falls into after your salary. If your salary already uses the personal allowance, your gig profit is taxed from the first pound at 20% (or 40% if it pushes you over £50,270). Use the PAYE toggle in the calculator above.
Rough guide: profit under £12,570 — set aside nothing. £12,570 to £25,000 — around 20–22% per month. £25,000 to £50,000 — around 26–30%. The calculator above gives you the exact monthly figure. Transfer that amount to a separate savings account every time you are paid by the platform.
